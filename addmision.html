TNEA 2026 Admissions

Information Portal for Other State Candidates

Overview

For TNEA 2026, students from other states can apply via a limited "Other State Quota" (approximately 5% of seats). Candidates must register online during the specified May-June window.

Admissions are primarily based on 12th Board Marks (PCM). While government quota seats are limited, management quota seats offer broader access for all candidates.

Eligibility for Other State Students

  • Nativity Certificate: You generally need a valid nativity certificate from the relevant authority to prove your origin.
  • Residency Requirement: Students who completed Class 8-12 in Tamil Nadu OR Tamil Nadu natives who studied outside TN are eligible for the main TNEA counseling.
  • Children of Govt. Employees: Children of Central Govt. or All India Service officers working in Tamil Nadu are also eligible to apply.

Application Process

  1. No Separate Exam: TNEA is a counseling process based on Class 12 marks. There is no specific entrance test conducted by the state.
  2. Register Online: Visit the official website www.tneaonline.org during the application period (typically May/June).
  3. Select Quota: Apply under the "Other State Quota" for government college seats or opt for the management quota in private colleges.
  4. Required Documents: Prepare your 12th mark sheet (Physics, Chemistry, Maths), nativity/residency proof, and other relevant certificates.
